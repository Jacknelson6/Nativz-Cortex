# Video Scripting Framework

## Negativity Wins
Negativity content catches people's eye because it provokes stronger feelings than positive content does.
- Indirectly challenge mistakes or misconceptions (e.g., "Squats just suck for growing your legs, try these instead.")
- Avoid insulting the viewer directly. Focus on the mistake, not the person.
- Use negativity carefully — spark interest, not offense.

## The You-Format
Personalization warrants attention. The closer you get to a "1-on-1" feel, the more engaging your content becomes.
1. Replace the Plural: Instead of "people," "everyone," or "you guys," use "you."
   - "Everyone is looking for ways to save money" → "You're looking for ways to save money."
2. Give the Ownership: Instead of "the dog," say "your dog." Instead of "the bank account," say "your bank account."
   - "When you finish the sales call…" → "When you finish your sales call…"

## Script Rules
- As short as possible. Only as long as it needs to be while still solving the viewer's problem.
- Remove unnecessary context. Get straight to the main point.
- As simple as possible. Most viewers read at around a 7th-grade level.
- Don't dumb down info, just present it clearly.

## Idea Generation Techniques

### Technique 1: Trend Jacking
Jump on a topic that's already gaining momentum. Platforms want to serve more content around trending topics, and audiences are actively searching for takes.
- Monitor: TikTok search suggestions, Reddit /r/popular, Twitter/X Explore, Google Trends, LinkedIn
- Speed is everything. Shoot the video that day. Being first > being polished.
- Connect it to your niche creatively. The more unexpected the link, the more it stands out.

### Technique 2: Social Hacking
Borrow attention from big names, brands, or institutions with larger followings.
- Pick someone with a loyal audience (creators, CEOs, celebrities, companies)
- Say something NEW about them — reframe through your lens
- Find crossover ideas (social hacking + trend jacking = powerful combo)

### Technique 3: Pre-Validated Content
If something already blew up somewhere else, it's proven audiences care.
- Reddit: Sort niche subreddits by "Top" > "All Time" or "This Month." Thousands of upvotes = validated idea.
- Google Alerts: Set for niche topics, get daily digests before most creators see them.
- LinkedIn + Twitter: Track high-performing posts, strike when something gains traction.

### Technique 4: Reverse Engineering Viral Formats
Every viral video leaves clues. Break down what made it work.
1. Pick a high-performing video (500K+ views, 8%+ engagement)
2. Transcribe it — handwrite for deeper internalization
3. Label each section: Hook, Idea, Context, Body, Outro
4. Rate each section 1-10
5. Mark storytelling devices: curiosity, humor, stakes, unusual phrasing, surprise, metaphors

### Technique 5: Visual & Editing Analysis
- Track every edit by timestamp (visuals, zooms, cuts, SFX, captions)
- Count edits per second/minute as a benchmark
- Watch for patterns: shot frequency, zoom timing, caption styles, SFX on beats
- Build your own editing template matching their intensity

### Technique 6: "Pope in the Pool" (Visual Pacifiers)
Distract the viewer's visual brain while feeding them complex info.
- Pair educational voiceovers with stimulating visuals: cooking, gym footage, driving, nature
- Avoid overused clips unless audience expects that style

### Technique 7: Templatize Your Own Virality
When your video pops off, study it like a scientist.
1. List everything that contributed: hook phrasing, music, captions, camera, energy/tone
2. Swap only the variables (topic, stat, visual) — keep structure intact
3. Match the energy/delivery that made the original hit

## Summary
- Create a daily input system for trends, alerts, and ideas
- Act fast on trend jacking opportunities
- Use social hacking to borrow attention from bigger audiences
- Templatize and systematize content that works
- Break down content at the transcript and visual level
- Use editing cadence and visual pacifiers to keep people watching
